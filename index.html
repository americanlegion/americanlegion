Chaplain's Corner

Communication and Harmony

Posted on September 7, 2015

This morning at our worship service, the theme for my message was communication and harmony, both with our work for the Lord and also with each other.

The scripture for the message was Philippians 4:1-9. Paul mentions how the two women, in the scripture, struggled beside him in the work of the gospel. Paul communicated from his prison cell the work of the Lord. He stood firm in that work, knowing it had to be done in harmony in order for the work to be successful.

Communication and harmony are keys in all we plan and do to reach our goals. The American Legion Auxiliary is no exception to using these for our own work. We have many goals that have been set, goals that are necessary in our organization if we are to successfully support our Veterans.

Lack of communication and lack of harmony break down the process we go through to reach the goals we set. Relationships become broken; anger and hurt feelings come into play and slow down the process and in some cases stop the process all together.

Think of where we would be today if our Veterans did not communicate well with those around them during their time in the service. Where would we be if they could not work together in harmony? Our Veterans knew that in order to win the war there had to be good communication and they had to work well together in harmony.

As we go through this ALA year, no matter what position we hold in the ALA, keep good communications as one of our goals and always work in harmony with our ALA sisters. Our goal is not for self; our goal is for our Veterans.

For God and Country,
